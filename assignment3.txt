1. What are Public and Private IP Addresses?
Public IP:
Globally unique, assigned by your ISP.
Can be accessed over the internet.
Example: 172.217.10.46 (Google’s server IP).
Private IP:
Used within a private network (home, office, LAN).
Not routable over the internet.
Example: 192.168.1.10 (your laptop or phone).
2. Private IP Address Ranges (RFC 1918)
Range	                        Subnet Mask	Notes
10.0.0.0 – 10.255.255.255	255.0.0.0	Large private networks
172.16.0.0 – 172.31.255.255	255.240.0.0	Medium private networks
192.168.0.0 – 192.168.255.255	255.255.0.0	Common for home networks
Public IP vs Private Network Access
Your public IP is what the internet sees — for example, 103.45.23.10.
Your private network (home Wi-Fi, office LAN) has devices like 192.168.1.5 or 192.168.1.10 behind your router.

Even if someone knows your public IP:

They can try to connect to your network, but most home routers have firewalls that block unsolicited incoming connections.
They cannot directly see your private IP addresses or access devices inside your LAN.
