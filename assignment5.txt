2. Class D IP Addresses (224–239)
Purpose: Multicast
Used to send packets to a group of devices at once, instead of one-to-one.
Example: Streaming a live video to multiple clients.
Not used for regular hosts.
Structure:
First 4 bits: 1110
Remaining 28 bits: define multicast group
Example addresses:
224.0.0.1 → All hosts on the local network
233.1.2.3 → Specific multicast group
3. Class E IP Addresses (240–255)
Purpose: Experimental / Future use
Reserved for research, testing, or future use.
Not assigned to hosts or networks on the internet.
Structure:
First 4 bits: 1111
Example addresses:
240.0.0.1, 255.255.255.255
Note:
255.255.255.255 is technically a broadcast address, used for network-wide messages.
